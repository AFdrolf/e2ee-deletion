\section{Introduction}

- E2EE gold standard and well understood.

- In recent years, they have become feature rich.

- One such feature is general class of message deletion.

- Do these actually provide privacy?


For intro : Privacy in end-to-end encrypted (E2EE) messaging traditionally equates with (just) confidentiality in transit: security holds against adversaries that observe encrypted payloads, but compromise of endpoint devices is out of scope---after all, an adversary with device access can simply read all messages to begin with. Modern E2EE applications, however, now offer a wide array of \emph{message deletion} features, such as unsending messages, closing conversations, view-once media, and more. These features provide an extra layer of privacy, allowing users to safeguard sensitive messages from adversaries who gain access to their device.
